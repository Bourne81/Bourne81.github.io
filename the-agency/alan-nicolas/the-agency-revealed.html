The Methodology

CIA + DLI + FSI
// intelligence source

The Agency's methodology is not academic. It is drawn from three of the most effective language training programmes ever built — the CIA, the Defense Language Institute, and the Foreign Service Institute. Not because we romanticise intelligence work, but because these systems were designed to produce results under pressure. That is the only context that matters.

// method I
CIA
Language utility over mastery. The 1,000 highest-frequency words cover 80% of conversation. Every element must serve a mission-specific context. Emotional anchoring through high-stakes scenarios only.
// method II
DLI
Military precision through pattern drilling. 50–100 repetitions per structure until it fires without thought. Job-specific vocabulary. Native speaker models. Contextual immersion.
// method III
FSI
Diplomatic communication under pressure. Audio-lingual drilling — pattern over explanation. Behavioural conditioning: repetition equals automaticity. Practical over theoretical. Always.
